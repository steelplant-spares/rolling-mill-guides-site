Roller Entry & Exit Guides

Roller entry and exit guides for bar, section and wire rod mills. Available in 2-roller, 4-roller and 6-roller configurations for roughing, intermediate and finishing stands. Designed for accurate pass alignment, bite control and stable delivery.
Key Features
  • Single-point simultaneous roll gap adjustment
  • Nitrided and ground steel bearing pins and wear parts
  • High-capacity taper roller bearings with mill scale seals
  • Cast stainless steel, carbon steel or ductile iron construction
  • Available for 12mm to 300mm product size range

Guide Support Arms

Support arm assemblies for mounting roller entry and exit guides onto the mill stand rest bar. Designed for rigid positioning and quick changeover during roll pass changes.

Multi-Strand Slitting Guides

Slitting guides for multi-strand rebar rolling lines. Available in 2-slit, 3-slit and 5-strand configurations for entry and exit positions. Designed for stable strand separation, accurate pass alignment and reduced cobble risk during high-speed slit rolling.
Key Features
  • Cast stainless steel guide body
  • Simple single-point simultaneous roller gap adjustment
  • High-capacity taper roller bearings for bar mill operation
  • Quick-change holder mounting with FPA (Fine Pass Adjustment)
  • Available in 2, 3, 4 and 5-strand configurations

Friction & Static Guides

Sliding and friction guides for roughing and intermediate mill stands. Used at low-speed or high-impact positions where sliding contact is preferred. Includes entry static guides, delivery strippers, trough guides and pipe strippers.
Key Features

  • Hardened tool steel wear inserts at nose and wear points
  • Adjustable width with tie-bolt or Rocker Clamp system
  • Replaceable nosepieces for various pass profiles
  • Available in single to quintuple strand arrangements

Common problems

Common guide problems in rolling mills

A guide problem is not always a material problem. It may come from alignment, cooling, lubrication, bearing selection, roll pass matching or installation condition.

ProblemPossible causesWhat we need to checkReplacement direction
Cobble before standEntry guide misalignment, wrong roller gap, bent bar head, roll pass mismatch.Stand number, entry side photo, video, product size and rolling speed.Check entry guide, bellmouth, centerline and roller opening.
Cobble after standExit guide wear, tail swing, next stand mismatch, unstable delivery.Exit guide photo, next stand distance, rolling speed, cobble position.Check exit guide, delivery guide and next stand entry condition.
Fast guide roller wearWrong material, poor cooling, scale abrasion, small gap, eccentric loading.Used roller groove photo, service life, product size, water condition.Check guide roller material, groove profile and heat treatment.
Bearing failurePoor lubrication, water entering, scale entering, high temperature, excessive speed.Bearing model, failed bearing photo, lubrication method, cooling layout.Check bearing, seal, shaft fit, cooling and lubrication design.
Surface scratchesWorn guide plate, seized roller, scale stuck inside guide, wrong opening.Scratch position, continuous/intermittent mark, worn guide photos.Replace guide plates / rollers, adjust gap and clean scale path.
Twist instabilityWrong twist angle, worn twist roller, centerline issue, low bar temperature.Pass sequence, twist guide drawing, product size, current problem video.Check twist guide roller profile, angle and centerline.
Guide position

Entry guide controls biting. Exit guide stabilizes delivery.

The roll pass changes the steel shape. The guide makes sure the hot bar enters the pass on center and leaves the stand in a stable direction.

Entry side

Before the roll pass

Entry guides hold the hot bar on the pass centerline and help the bar bite into the rolls without side swing.

Roll pass

Inside the stand

The roll groove changes the bar section. Wrong entry angle, guide gap or centerline can cause cobble, scratches or uneven wear.

Exit side

After the roll pass

Exit and delivery guides stabilize the bar after deformation and keep the next stand entry direction under control.
